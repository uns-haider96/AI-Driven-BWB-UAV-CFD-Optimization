# Wind Tunnel Testing

The manufactured BWB UAV prototype was tested in a wind tunnel to observe aerodynamic force and moment behavior under controlled flow conditions.

## Test Purpose

The wind tunnel testing stage was used to connect the CFD-based design process with experimental aerodynamic evaluation. The test setup allowed the prototype to be mounted in the flow and evaluated across multiple cases involving different wind speeds and angles.

## Measured Responses

The experimental result summary includes maximum lift, maximum drag, side force, pitch response, yaw response, and roll response.

![Wind tunnel force and moment results](../assets/figures/wind-tunnel/01-wind-tunnel-force-and-moment-results.jpg)

## Interpretation

The wind tunnel results provide experimental evidence that the manufactured BWB prototype was physically tested after the CFD and optimization stages. The tests are best presented as experimental trend validation and prototype-level aerodynamic evaluation rather than as perfect one-to-one CFD validation.

The force and moment plots show the aerodynamic response across multiple wind-speed and angle cases. Lift, drag, side force, pitch, yaw, and roll behavior were all recorded, giving a broader view of the prototype response than lift and drag alone.

## Engineering Value

This stage strengthens the project because it demonstrates a complete engineering workflow: computational design, surrogate-model-based optimization, composite manufacturing, physical prototype realization, wind tunnel testing, and experimental aerodynamic interpretation.

## Reporting Caution

For public documentation, the wind tunnel plots should be described as prototype-level experimental results. Avoid claiming full CFD validation unless force-balance calibration, repeatability, uncertainty bounds, sign conventions, and test-condition matching are fully documented.
